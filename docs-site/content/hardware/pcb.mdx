# PCB

KiCad design + assembly for the Nudge Controller main board.

<Callout type="warning">
**Pin assignments are not final.** The 3rd matrix row (`D15` / P1.13) and the
encoder pins (P0.31 / P0.29 / P0.02) are placeholders until verified against a
physical nice!nano v2 and the KiCad netlist. The PCB and the firmware
[overlay](/firmware/setup) must agree before either is trusted.
</Callout>

## Tools

- [KiCad 7+](https://www.kicad.org/) (free, open source)
- The [nice!nano footprint/symbol](https://nicekeyboards.com/docs/nice-nano/)
  (Pro Micro–compatible) added as a library

## Schematic outline

1. **nice!nano socket** — Pro Micro pinout. Break out the matrix + encoder GPIO,
   plus `3V3`, `GND`, `B+`/`B-`.
2. **Key matrix** — 3 rows × 7 cols = 21 switches, one **1N4148** per key,
   cathode toward the row (`col2row`).
   - Rows: `D4` (P0.28), `D5` (P0.30), `D15` (P1.13)
   - Cols: `D6` (P1.11), `D7` (P0.06), `D8` (P0.08), `D9` (P0.17), `D10` (P0.20), `D16` (P0.10), `D14` (P1.12)
3. **Encoder** — Alps EC11K: A (P0.31), B (P0.29), C → GND, SW (P0.02). Add
   **10 kΩ pull-ups** A→3V3 and B→3V3.
4. **Power** — JST-PH 2.0 mm to `B+`/`B-`, with a slide switch inline on `B+`.

## Layout tips

- Keep encoder A/B traces short and away from the nice!nano antenna corner.
- Leave the antenna corner free of copper pour.
- Reserve a battery-pocket keep-out (cell not under pins).
- Add mounting holes aligned with the case + switch plate.

## Assembly order

1. **Diodes (21×)** — orientation matters; cathode (black band) toward the row.
   A reversed diode makes a whole row/column misbehave.
2. **Encoder + pull-ups** — solder the EC11K, then the two 10 kΩ resistors on A/B.
3. **Switches + plate** — drop switches through the [plate](/hardware/case) first,
   seat onto the PCB, then solder so everything stays square.
4. **nice!nano** — sockets recommended (reusable); mind the USB-C orientation.

## Exporting Gerbers (JLCPCB / PCBWay)

1. **File → Plot**, format **Gerber**. Layers: `F.Cu`, `B.Cu`, `F.Mask`,
   `B.Mask`, `F.SilkS`, `B.SilkS`, `Edge.Cuts`.
2. **Generate Drill Files** (Excellon, PTH+NPTH).
3. Zip the output folder and upload; 1.6 mm FR4, HASL or ENIG, batch of 5.

Continue to [Case & Plate](/hardware/case).
