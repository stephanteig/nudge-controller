# Case & Plate

A 3D-printed two-part shell plus a laser-cut switch plate.

<Callout type="error">
**LiPo safety.** Lithium cells can vent or ignite if shorted, punctured, or
reverse-wired. Check polarity against the nice!nano silkscreen (**red = +,
black = −**), never charge unattended, and stop immediately if a cell puffs or
heats up.
</Callout>

<Callout type="info">
The CAD/STL/DXF files aren't in the repo yet — their dimensions derive from the
[PCB](/hardware/pcb) outline, so finalise the board first.
</Callout>

## Parts

| Part | Process | Material | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Top shell | 3D print | PLA / PETG | Encoder hole + keycap openings |
| Bottom shell | 3D print | PLA / PETG | Battery pocket, USB-C cutout, switch slot |
| Switch plate | Laser cut | 1.5 mm acrylic / FR4 | 14 × 14 mm switch cutouts |

## 3D printing

- **Material:** PLA indoors; **PETG** for warm edit bays (PLA softens with heat).
- **Layer height:** 0.2 mm · **Walls:** 3 perimeters · **Infill:** 20–30%.
- **Supports:** only under the USB-C and encoder overhangs.

### Cutouts to include

- **USB-C** aligned to the nice!nano edge
- **Encoder** hole for the EC11K bushing (~Ø7 mm) + knob clearance
- **Power switch** slot on a side wall
- **M2** screw bosses / heat-set insert holes matching the PCB

## Laser-cut switch plate

- MX cutout = **14.0 × 14.0 mm** (use Choc spacing for Kailh Choc).
- Match the 3×7 key pitch to the PCB (typically 19.05 mm for MX).
- 1.5 mm thickness seats MX clips correctly.
- Export the plate as **DXF**.

## Assembly

1. Seat PCB + plate into the bottom shell.
2. Route the battery into its pocket (away from pins, a little slack).
3. Fit the encoder knob through the top; attach the top shell.
4. Screw together with M2 hardware.

## First power-on

1. Flip the power switch on.
2. Pair **"Nudge Controller"** from your host's Bluetooth menu.
3. Open a text editor and confirm input, then try DaVinci Resolve.

If nothing appears, see [Firmware → Flashing](/firmware/flashing) troubleshooting.
